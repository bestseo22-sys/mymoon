The Truth About Hiring Packers & Movers in India

Every year, millions of families shift homes across Indian cities. And every year, thousands face the same painful experience — delayed trucks, broken furniture, surprise charges at delivery, or a mover who disappears after taking the advance.

The problem isn't that good movers don't exist. The problem is that the old way of finding them — searching "packers and movers near me," calling 10 numbers, getting 10 different stories — gives you no way to verify who you're actually dealing with. Anyone can sound professional on the phone.

MyMoon was built to fix this. We pre-verify every mover on our platform — checking their business registration, customer reviews, equipment, and operational track record before they can accept a single booking. You don't just get a phone number. You get a professional with a reputation to protect.

What Separates a Reliable Mover from a Bad One

  • Proper packing materials: Quality movers use multi-layered packing — bubble wrap, foam, corrugated boxes. Cheap movers use newspaper and hope for the best.
  • Written itemised quote: A professional mover gives you a cost breakdown before starting. An unverified one gives you a verbal number and changes it at delivery.
  • Transit insurance: Accidents happen. A reliable mover either carries insurance or makes it easy for you to add transit cover.
  • GPS-tracked vehicles: On intercity moves, you should know where your belongings are — not be left wondering for 48 hours.
  • No full advance demand: Paying 20–30% to book a date is normal. Paying 100% upfront before loading is a red flag.

Every mover on MyMoon meets these standards before joining our platform. That's our entry requirement, not a marketing claim.

Mistakes People Make When Hiring Movers

  • Choosing by price alone: The cheapest quote almost always hides extras — for stairs, packing materials, heavy items, or "special handling." Always ask for a fully itemised quote.
  • Not checking reviews: Any mover can sound professional on a call. Customer reviews tell you what actually happens on moving day when no one's on their best behaviour.
  • Booking too late: Good movers fill up fast, especially around month-end, Diwali, and January. Book at least 5–7 days out for local moves, 2 weeks for intercity.
  • Skipping the inventory list: Before loading, photograph all items and make a written list. This protects you if anything goes missing or arrives damaged.
  • Signing delivery without checking: Before you sign the completion receipt, inspect every major item. Once signed, claims become very difficult.
  • Not asking about insurance: Standard charges don't include transit insurance. For electronics and expensive furniture, adding it is almost always worth the small extra cost.

Local vs. Intercity Moves — Key Differences

Local moves (within the same city, under 30 km) are usually priced by the hour or as a flat rate. The truck is smaller, the journey is short, and the whole move typically wraps up in half a day.

Intercity moves are priced based on weight or volume of goods plus distance. You'll have a full-size truck, a 2–4 person crew, and a journey that can take 12–48 hours. GPS tracking and transit insurance matter much more for these moves. MyMoon has specialists for both.

Pro Advice

10 Tips for a Stress-Free Move

1
Book at least a week early

Good movers fill up fast — especially at month-end and weekends. Book early for better availability and rates.

2
Declutter before the move

Less stuff = less packing = lower cost. Donate, sell, or discard items you don't need before movers arrive.

3
Photograph everything before packing

Photo all belongings, especially electronics and furniture. This is your evidence if anything gets damaged.

4
Create an inventory list

List every item you're moving. Ask the mover to sign this. At delivery, check against the list before signing.

5
Get a written, itemised quote

Never agree to a verbal-only quote. Ask for a written breakdown of packing, transport, and labour charges separately.

6
Defrost your refrigerator the night before

Fully defrost and dry the fridge before moving day. Inform your mover in advance if you need help with disconnection.

7
Keep essentials with you

Pack medicines, chargers, documents, and valuables in a personal bag. Keep this with you — not in the truck.

8
Inform your society in advance

Most housing societies need 48–72 hours advance notice for shifting. Inform your RWA early to avoid last-minute delays.

9
Update your address before moving

Aadhaar, bank accounts, gas, insurance — start updating your address a week before. Some take 7–14 days to process.

10
Don't pay full amount until delivery

Pay 20–30% advance to confirm booking. Pay the balance only after goods arrive and you've inspected them at the new location.

Frequently Asked Questions

How soon will I get a callback?

Is there any fee to use MyMoon?
Completely free. MyMoon does not charge customers anything for getting quotes or connecting with movers. You only pay the mover directly for their service. No commissions, no registration fees.
How many quotes will I receive?
Typically 2–3 quotes from different verified movers in your area. Enough to compare pricing without being overwhelmed by calls from 20 vendors.
What if my belongings get damaged?
Document any damage with photos before signing the delivery receipt. If you have transit insurance, file a claim. Without insurance, raise it with the mover immediately. Contact MyMoon support if the mover is unresponsive — we help mediate all disputes for bookings made through our platform.
Do movers disassemble and reassemble furniture?
Most movers handle standard furniture disassembly/reassembly (beds, wardrobes, tables) either included or as an add-on. For modular kitchens and built-in wardrobes, a separate carpenter may be needed. Confirm when you get the quote.
Can I transport my car or bike?
Yes. Vehicle transport is available as an add-on or standalone booking. Cars go on enclosed or open carriers depending on distance. Bikes are crated for long-distance moves. Mention vehicle transport in the notes field when you submit.
What is the best time of month to move?
The 10th–20th of the month is the least busy and often 15–25% cheaper. Month-end (28th–3rd) is peak — most rental agreements end on the 1st. Weekdays are also cheaper and less stressful than weekends.
How is intercity move pricing calculated?
Intercity moves are priced by weight or cubic volume of goods plus distance. Additional charges include packing materials, loading/unloading labour, tolls, and insurance if opted. A good mover gives you a written itemised quote that covers all of this. Never accept a "total only" quote without a breakdown.
